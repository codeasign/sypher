- [Lists](../lists/overview) вҖ” scatter plot coordinates come from lists or arrays

## Why This Matters

Data visualization is how you understand your data. Summary statistics hide distributions, outliers, and trends. A histogram of model confidence scores shows you the spread that a mean value collapses. A scatter plot of accuracy vs latency reveals tradeoffs you would miss in a table. A line chart of training loss over epochs tells you whether your model is learning, overfitting, or stuck.

In AI engineering, you will use plots constantly: to inspect evaluation results, compare model performance, debug training curves, and communicate findings to stakeholders. matplotlib is the foundational library вҖ” pandas, seaborn, and many other tools build on it.

## The Problem

You trained three model variants and have their accuracy over 100 evaluation batches. The raw numbers are columns in a CSV. You need to see whether model B converges faster, whether model C is unstable, and where the accuracy plateau begins. You cannot see these patterns in a table вҖ” you need a line plot.

## The Concept

matplotlib has two interfaces. The **pyplot** interface (`plt.plot()`, `plt.xlabel()`) is quick and interactive вҖ” good for exploration. The **object-oriented** interface (`fig, ax = plt.subplots()`; `ax.plot()`) gives you explicit control over every element in the figure and is the recommended approach for production code. This lesson uses the object-oriented interface.
